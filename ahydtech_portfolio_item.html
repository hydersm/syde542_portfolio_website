Ahydtech Website

Quick Description

Ahydtech Geomorphic is a Canadian company created by my father, Bahar SM, and located in Guelph, Ontario. Ahydtech provides engineering consulting services in field management, numerical modeling, coastal engineering, municipal environment assessment, hydrologic modeling and hydraulic modeling.

During the initial phases of the company, my father created a basic website to help advertise his new firm. However, he quickly found out that the website was poorly designed, and as a result it did not attract many customers. So he asked me to redesign the website for him. Thus, this page describes the design and implementation process of the new website for Ahydtech.

User Requirements

Interview

To gather requirements, I first interviewed the site owner. The questions below were asked. The answers were recorded in point form.

  • What is Ahydtech?
    • Ahydtech Geomorphic is a Canadian company located in Guelph, Ontario
    • We provide services in field measurement, hydrologic and hydraulic modeling, numerical modeling, coastal engineering and municipal environmental Assessment
  • What is the purpose of the website?
    • A place where people can find out about the company (goals, objectives, experiences and services)
    • Advertise the services and products offered by the company
    • Allow customers to contact company staff
  • Who are the users?
    • Public offices (municipalities)
    • Conservation authorities
    • Provincial and federal governments
    • Private land owner and land developers
  • What are the user’s needs? Limitations? Preferences?
    • They want to see whether the company is capable of doing the job (they look at experience, staff, qualifications)
  • What are the users’ tasks? Which are important?
    • Navigation (very important)
    • Read information (ease of finding and reading information is important)
    • Contact company staff (very important)
    • Purchase services (this is not very important at the moment, payment can be done through other services)
  • Is there anything you would like to mention for the new site?
    • I would like to maintain the 2-tier navigation structure (it helps me organize content better)
    • I want the website to reflect the fact that the company is "young"

Requirements and Constraints

Through doing the interviews, the following requirements and constraints were determined:

Requirements:

  • Has to contain information about:
    • The company
    • Project experience
    • Services the company provides
    • Software the company uses / provides training for
    • Contact information
  • Has to have a 2-tier navigation structure
  • Has to be easy to navigate
  • Has to be appealing (has to make the company look "young")

Constraints:

  • Has to be a website
  • Has to be lightweight (the website can not involve a large download)
  • Has to be easy to change (the html/css/js should be readable and well-structured)
  • Has to be compatible on all browses
  • Does not need a separate mobile version, but the website should somewhat scale down for smaller screens

Layout and Navigation

Navigation Hierarchy

Based on the requirements and the hierarchy on the old website, the following hierachy was determined. The hierarchy was generated using the card-sorting technique. In this technique, elements are created beforehand and the user is allowed to organize into groups. This generates a structure that is natural to the user. Early Design Techniques

Moodboards

After determining the general content hierarchy and layout of the website, I began experimenting with various "themes" using moodboards. Moodboards define the theme of the website. It can contain color palettes, shapes, fonts and etc. It, however, does not contain content or hierarchy. Three moodboards were generated and compared. The three moodboard is shown below. The first moodboard is designed to evoke an "engineering" theme. It has CAD drawings, watershed models and engineering tools such as wrenches and rulers. The second moodboard is designed to eboke a "nature" theme. It has trees, mountains and fonts that cater towards nature. The third moodboard is "cartoon" themed. It evokes the feel of a start-up.

(1) An "engineering" themed moodboard.

(2) A "nature" themed moodboard.

(3) A "cartoon" and "nature" themed moodboard.

It was decided that the third moodboard will be used because it evoke the theme of a start-up. It conveys that the company is relatively new and contemporary.

Wireframe

After choosing a moodboard, a wireframe was generated. A wireframe is a drawing that shows the page layout, navigation and skeleton. It does not show the font choises, colour, logos, images or full text. The generated wireframe is shown below:

Wireframe showing the front page.

Design Refinement

After the wireframe, design refinements must be made. These refinements include the font choice, logo and colour.

Font

It was decided that website's font should be Lato. There are several reasons for this:

  • It is open source
  • It is an easy-to-read font without too many distractions
  • It feels contemporary and matches the theme of the third moodboard

Logo

The logo was generated by the third party. The moodboard and wireframe were provided to the artist to help with the logo generation. The resulting logo is shown below:

Colour

Several considers were made with respect to colour. The main points are listed below:

  • Color economy: use one main colour for the whole website - rgb(31, 96, 130)
  • Salience: apply colours in areas that need attention (headers, footers and subsections)
  • Experiment with whitespace: add whitespace to remove clutter

Evaluation

After the implementation, the website was tested for usability. Usability testing is a technique used in user-centered interaction design to evaluate a product by testing it on users. Our usability test conducted by having the user perform three tasks and the usability was measured using feedback forms. The three tasks are as follows:

  • Find the contact information for Ahydtech.
  • Find Ahydtech's project experience
  • Find the list of software being used by Ahydtech.

The result of the usability test is shown below. As it can be seen, most of the feedback was positive indicating that the website is easily to use and appealing.
