Ahydtech Website

Quick Description

Ahydtech Geomorphic is a Canadian company created by my father, Bahar SM, and located in Guelph, Ontario. Ahydtech provides engineering consulting services in field management, numerical modeling, coastal engineering, municipal environment assessment, hydrologic modeling and hydraulic modeling.

During the initial phases of the company, my father created a basic website to help advertise his new firm. However, he quickly found out that the website was poorly designed, and as a result it did not attract many customers. So he asked me to redesign the website for him. Thus, this page describes the design and implementation process of the new website for Ahydtech.

User Requirements

Interview

To gather requirements, I first interviewed the site owner. The questions below were asked. The answers were recorded in point form.

  • What is Ahydtech?
    • Ahydtech Geomorphic is a Canadian company located in Guelph, Ontario
    • We provide services in field measurement, hydrologic and hydraulic modeling, numerical modeling, coastal engineering and municipal environmental Assessment
  • What is the purpose of the website?
    • A place where people can find out about the company (goals, objectives, experiences and services)
    • Advertise the services and products offered by the company
    • Allow customers to contact company staff
  • Who are the users?
    • Public offices (municipalities)
    • Conservation authorities
    • Provincial and federal governments
    • Private land owner and land developers
  • What are the user’s needs? Limitations? Preferences?
    • They want to see whether the company is capable of doing the job (they look at experience, staff, qualifications)
  • What are the users’ tasks? Which are important?
    • Navigation (very important)
    • Read information (ease of finding and reading information is important)
    • Contact company staff (very important)
    • Purchase services (this is not very important at the moment, payment can be done through other services)
  • Is there anything you would like to mention for the new site?
    • I would like to maintain the 2-tier navigation structure (it helps me organize content better)
    • I want the website to reflect the fact that the company is "young"

Requirements and Constraints

Through doing the interviews, the following requirements and constraints were determined:

Requirements:

  • Has to contain information about:
    • The company
    • Project experience
    • Services the company provides
    • Software the company uses / provides training for
    • Contact information
  • Has to have a 2-tier navigation structure
  • Has to be easy to navigate
  • Has to be appealing (has to make the company look "young")

Constraints:

  • Has to be a website
  • Has to be lightweight (the website can not involve a large download)
  • Has to be easy to change (the html/css/js should be readable and well-structured)
  • Has to be compatible on all browses
  • Does not need a separate mobile version, but the website should somewhat scale down for smaller screens
