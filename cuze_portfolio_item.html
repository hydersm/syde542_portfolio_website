Cuze iOS and Android Video Game

Quick Description

A couple months ago, a friend approached me with an idea: a 3D puzzle-based game. As an avid gamer myself, I thought it would be interesting to give this idea a jab.

So over the course of 2 months, I worked with this friend to design and implement the game. We wanted to make this game feel unique and challending. The game is now fully implemented and is pending on being published to the iOS and Android application stores. This page describes the process we went through to arrive to the final product.

User Requirements

Personas

The first step was to develop a couple personas. A persona is a fictional character created to represent a user type that might use a site, brand, or product in a similar way. Making persona will help us get a better understanding of our potential users and will help us generate application requirements:

Hydrabad

  • He is a 22 year old undergraduate student at a university. He regularly take a bus to class and back.
  • During the short free times he has (bus rides, class breaks, etc), he would like play something to relieve his stress.
  • He would like the game to be mentally challenging and engaging.
  • He is a competitive person, so he would like the ability to share his score with his friends.
  • He also wants a battery-saving feature so that he could save some juice for other tasks.
  • Due to the high possibility of interruptions, he would like the ability to pause the game.

Jordan

  • He is a 7 year old kid who borrows his parent's phone to sometimes play games at home.
  • His parents are mostly okay with this, but they are worried that the kid might accidently purchase in-game items or may come across some inappropriate content.
  • He tends to enjoy action-based games with alot of explosions and power ups, but his parents would like him to play a game that is mentally engaging.
  • Previously, he really enjoyed games that used the phone's gyroscope (where tilting the phone had an effect on the game).
  • He really enjoys unlocking things in games. Otherwise, there is not motivation to play.

Requirements and Constraints

Using the personas, the following requirements and constraints were determined:

Requirements:

  • The game should be playable in short durations
  • Regardless of where the player is in the game, the player should be able to pause the game.
  • The game should be mentally challending and engaging.
  • The game should have a leaderboard.
  • The game should have a battery saving feature.
  • The game must not have any inappropriate content towards any kind of audience (especially kids).
  • The game should have exciting animations
  • For the first release, it is not neccessary to offer in-game purchases. However, if it is implemented, it must be child-proof.
  • If possible, the game should give the player to control some aspects of the game with a gyroscope.
  • The game should have unlockable content to give players some sense of "progression".

Constraints:

  • It must be a mobile game. This has several implications:
    • It must take less than 100MB of disk space
    • The game will have limited internet access due it being a mobile app.
    • The hardware (CPU and GPU) powering the application will be limited.
    • The game must additionally comply with Apple's and Google's app store constraints.
  • The game implementation has to be done thoughtfully, so that it can be improved later down the line.

Game Design

Before designing the app, I first wanted to design the game. To do this, I explored different options and picked the best based on the requirements and constraints.

Maze Type

I first explored different maze types. The first maze is inspired from a common toy. It is a maze "wrapped" around a cube. The third maze is based on platform. This can be seen in various video games. The third kind of maze is a complicated maze embedded into a sphere. All three types are shown below:

Maze wrapped around a cube.

A platform-based maze.

A maze embedded in a sphere.

It wad decided that the first type will be used. This is because the maze is common enough that many players will be able to understand it easily when its ported to a video game. Furthermore, we felt that cube maze is easily scalable to higher difficulties.

Player types

The next step is to determine the type of character the player will control. There are two options: either a ball or a human character. The two types of characters are shown below:

Ball player

Human player

It was decided that the "character" will be a ball. It makes the game relatable to the original physical toy.

Camera Types

The next step was to decide on the camera placement. The camera can either be placed right behind the character or from above (as shown below).

First-person camera

Overhead camera view

To help the users get a better view of the maze as a whole, the overhead camera was chosen.

Control mechanisms

Next, the control mechanism must be decided. This can be done in several ways: the ball can be controlled by either the phone gyroscope, a gamepad overlay, or a waypoint system. The three different mechanism are depicted below:

Gyroscope

Gamepad overlay

Waypoint system

To make the game feel unique and interesting, it was decided that the ball will by controlled using the phone's gyroscope.

Layout

After fully designing the main components of game, the application is design. The layout is first created. Since the application will use the gyroscope to control the ball, the application can only support one type of orientation (portrait or landscape). Otherwise, the phone will constantly change orientation while the user is tilting the phone due to the gyroscope. The two different layouts are shown below. Please note that layouts below are for the main gameplay screen (not other screen such as inventory and settings).

Portrait layout

Landscape layout

Since applications are naturally portrait and since most controls are accesible when the application is portrait, the portrait layout is chosen.
