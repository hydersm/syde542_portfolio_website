Cuze iOS and Android Video Game

Quick Description

A couple months ago, a friend approached me with an idea: a puzzle-based game where the player has to navigate through a maze wrapped around a cube. As an avid gamer myself, I thought it would be interesting to give this idea a jab.

So over the course of 2 months, I worked with this friend to design and implement the game. We wanted to make this game feel unique and challending. The game is now fully implemented and is pending on being published to the iOS and Android application stores. This page describes the process we went through to arrive to the final product.

User Requirements

Personas

The first step was to develop a couple personas. This will help us get a better understanding of out potential users:

Hydrabad

  • He is a 22 year old undergraduate student at a university. He regularly take a bus to class and back.
  • During the short free times he has (bus rides, class breaks, etc), he would like play something to relieve his stress.
  • He would like the game to be mentally challenging and engaging.
  • He is a competitive person, so he would like the ability to share his score with his friends.
  • He also wants a battery-saving feature so that he could save some juice for other tasks.
  • Due to the high possibility of interruptions, he would like the ability to pause the game.

Jordan

  • He is a 7 year old kid who borrows his parent's phone to sometimes play games at home.
  • His parents are mostly okay with this, but they are worried that the kid might accidently purchase in-game items or may come across some inappropriate content.
  • He tends to enjoy action-based games with alot of explosions and power ups, but his parents would like him to play a game that is mentally engaging.
  • Previously, he really enjoyed games that used the phone's gyroscope (where tilting the phone had an effect on the game).
  • He really enjoys unlocking things in games. Otherwise, there is not motivation to play.

Requirements and Constraints

Using the personas, the following requirements and constraints were determined:

Requirements:

  • The game should be playable in short durations
  • Regardless of where the player is in the game, the player should be able to pause the game.
  • The game should be mentally challending and engaging.
  • The game should have a leaderboard.
  • The game should have a battery saving feature.
  • The game must not have any inappropriate content towards any kind of audience (especially kids).
  • The game should have exciting animations
  • For the first release, it is not neccessary to offer in-game purchases. However, if it is implemented, it must be child-proof.
  • If possible, the game should give the player to control some aspects of the game with a gyroscope.
  • The game should have unlockable content to give players some sense of "progression".

Constraints:

  • It must be a mobile game. This has several implications:
    • It must take less than 100MB of disk space
    • The game will have limited internet access due it being a mobile app.
    • The hardware (CPU and GPU) powering the application will be limited.
    • The game must additionally comply with Apple's and Google's app store constraints.
  • The game implementation has to be done thoughtfully, so that it can be improved later down the line.
